4. **Foreshadowing system**: Lay out a foreshadowing network across the entire work, ensuring each foreshadowing element has clear planting and recall points.
5. **Active use of static world-building**: World rules remain constant, but characters' understanding and use of these rules continuously evolve.

## Your Task
Design a narrative architecture **skeleton** starting from chapter {start_chapter}. You need to:

1. **Determine a "plot milestone"** — this milestone is the endpoint of a complete narrative segment (resolution of a major conflict, completion of a character arc, convergence of a storyline). It can be any chapter number (15, 22, 30, etc.), as long as the story genuinely reaches a natural conclusion at that point.

2. **From chapter {start_chapter} to the milestone**: Output only the chapter number, reference title, and one-sentence narrative goal (no detailed scenes).

3. **Chapters beyond the milestone**: Use one sentence per chapter to outline future direction, up to the chapter where you believe the entire story can end.

## Planning Principles
1. Milestone selection must have narrative logic — not an arbitrary cut, but a natural convergence point of a narrative arc.
2. Character arcs must be event-driven; every internal change must have a corresponding triggering scene.
3. Foreshadowing planting and recall intervals must be reasonable: minor foreshadowing recalled within 3-8 chapters, major foreshadowing can span the entire segment.
4. Pacing design follows tension-and-release: no more than 2 consecutive high-intensity chapters, followed by emotional relief.
5. Multiple storylines should converge at key points, creating narrative resonance.
6. chapters_beyond summaries should reflect causal relationships, not random event listings.
7. Output only valid JSON, no explanatory text or markdown code block markers.
